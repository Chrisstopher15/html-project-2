Top 10 Most Dangerous Gangs part 1.

10.The Mungiki

The Mungiki have been terrorizing Kenyan citizens since 1980s with their brutal attacks against westernisation.Half-cult, half-cannibalistic gang, they are reported to have decapitated a two-year-old boy as part of a ritual. Banned by the government in 2002 for bludgeoning 20 people to death in a slum, they still have 100,000 members and their political affiliations allow them to carry out murder, extortion, and kidnappings. Around election time, this criminal network is called upon to intimidate voters by beheading people, amputating limbs, skinning heads, and drinking human blood.

9.Yamaguchi-Gumi

The biggest gang in the Yakuza with over 39,000 members, Yamaguchi-Gumi earn a massive $80 Billion every year. In the 1980s they waged a devastating 4-year street war with a breakaway gang that resulted in over 220 gun battles that left 36 gangsters dead. They even assasinated the mayor of Nagasaki in 2007 after a member's car was damaged at a public construction site. As a Yakuza organization, new members have to cut off all ties with family members and abide by the gruesome ritual of 'Yubitsume', when a gang member does something wrong, their fingers are chopped off.

8.Crips

The crips started out as a 60s civil rights group, but then morphed into a brutal street gang, which now has over 35,000 members across the U.S. In their 40-year feud with the Bloods, more than 15,000 people have been murdered and one of those victims is thought to be rapper Tupac Shakur. The LA times reported that notorious B.I.G. paid the crips $1 million to commit the crime, and a prime suspect in the case was Crips member Orlando Anderson who apparently fought with Tupac on the day of the murder. The case remains unsolved.

7.Bloods

The Bloods are Los Angeles street gang with a long-standing rivalry against enemy gang The Crips. Its 15,000 members live by the motto 'You kill one of mine, i'll kill one of yours' and they killed thousands of civilians caught in gang war crossfire. At the height the conflict Bloods were being offered up to $10,000 for each Crip killed. The rivalry goes so deep that the Bloods even target innocent people in drive-by shootings if they are wearing the affiliated color of the Crips.

6.Barrio Azteca

Barrio Azteca is a former Texan prison gang that boasts over 8,000 members and an extensive drug operation across central and northern America. Often hired as killers for larger criminal groups, according to U.S officials they were involved in more than 1,300 killings in the mexican city of Juarez in 2010. That year they also fatally shot 15 innocent teenagers at a house party, for no reason other than to assert power over their territory. Leader Eduardo Ravelo is currently on the FBI's top 10 most wanted list with a $100,000 reward.

5.Aryan Brotherhood

'Blood in, blood out'. That's the motto of the Aryan Brotherhood. A lethal, white supremacist prison gang from California. To get in, you have to kil a black or hispanic prisoner, try to leave and they'll kill you. One member who showed disloyalty had 5 of his fingers cut off with bolt cutters. The AB has around 20,000 members and it's reported that although this makes up 1/10 of the prison population, they are responsible for 30% of prison murders.

4.Primero Comando Da Capital

Founded in 1993 following the Carandiru massacre, where police killed over 100 prisoners. The PCC has 13,000 members and makes well over $2.5 million every month. This prison gang is so powerful that in May 2006 they managed to organize simultaneous rebellions in 79 state prisons and also held Sao Paulo under siege for almost a week while they burned government buildings and killed police officers. The carnage caused almost over 500 deaths. Thought to be Brazil's biggest criminal organization, almost 100 cops died in 2012 alone trying to combat the gang.

3.18th Street Gang.

The 18th streeet gang, or 'mara 18', is a hispanic organization in California and Central America, Everyday at least 2 people are assaulted by the group in LA alone, which is no surprise as its 20,000 members engage in murder, gang rape, and kidnappings. In 2007, footballer 'Wilson Palacios' 14 year-old brother was kidnapped and brutally murdered even after he paid $500,000 ransom. They attack family members of anyone who tries to leave, and if you show disrespect, expect a beating or even execution.

2.Mara Salvatrucha.

Rivals to the m18are the Mara Salvatrucha, a violent transnational force with over 70,000 members in the USA, Mexico, El Salvador, and even Canada, it's no surprise that the MS-13's motto is 'Rape, control, kill'. They recruit children as young as 10 and train them up to become hitmen and while boys are forcefully trained, girls younger than 12 are being exploited for child prostitution, druggged with weed and liquor before being sold to clients. The ongoing feud between Mara Salvatrucha and the M18 has left Honduras with the highest murder rate in the world with more than 20 murders every day. This is 19 times higher than the USA.

1.Los Zetas

Formed by a breakaway group of Mexico's Special Forces, Los Zetas are armed with assault riffles, anti-tank weapons and granade launchers. They are the most powerful group in the country's drug war that has already claimed 100,000 lives. Renowned for their violence, in 2011 they held up busses passing through San Fernando and forced the passangers to fight to the death killikng 193 people. Survivors of this gladiator fight were forced to join their gang. They even tortured and disemboweled two civilians for criticizing them on social media.
